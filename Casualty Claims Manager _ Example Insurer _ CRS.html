The Role

What you'll be doing

A broad, high-impact leadership position overseeing a technically complex casualty claims portfolio across multiple product lines.

Claims Leadership & Strategy

  • Lead the day-to-day management of a high-value casualty claims portfolio including General & Products Liability, Motor, and Professional Indemnity
  • Set the strategic direction for the claims function, driving technical excellence and consistency across the team
  • Provide a proactive, solution-focused approach that supports underwriters and builds broker confidence
  • Oversee complex, high-value, and contentious claims from first notification through to resolution
  • Develop and maintain strong relationships with external panel law firms, loss adjusters, and other specialists

People & Team Management

  • Lead, mentor, and develop a team of claims professionals, fostering a high-performance culture
  • Conduct regular performance reviews and identify training and development needs across the team
  • Act as a senior point of escalation for the team on complex coverage and liability questions
  • Collaborate closely with underwriting, actuarial, and legal teams on portfolio trends and emerging risks

Governance & Compliance

  • Ensure full compliance with Lloyd's and FCA regulatory requirements across all claims handling activity
  • Maintain robust claims handling procedures and controls in line with internal governance frameworks
  • Monitor reserving adequacy and work closely with actuarial on IBNR and claims development
  • Prepare and present claims MI and portfolio reporting to senior leadership

Market & Stakeholder Engagement

  • Represent the claims function in broker and client meetings, building trusted long-term relationships
  • Contribute to new business and renewal discussions by providing claims market insight
  • Stay abreast of legal, regulatory, and market developments relevant to casualty lines
  • Support the wider business on product development and wording reviews

Interview Process

What to expect

A transparent, respectful, and efficient process — typically completed within 3–4 weeks. Here's exactly what to expect at each stage.

1

Initial Conversation with CRS

~30–45 minutes · Phone or Video

A confidential, no-obligation conversation with Jamie Thomson at CRS. We'll talk through your background, what you're looking for, and why this opportunity could be the right fit. This is also your chance to ask questions about the role, the business, and the process before deciding to progress.

2

First Interview — HR & Competency

~60 minutes · Video call

A structured conversation with a member of the HR team and a senior hiring manager. Expect competency-based questions covering your leadership experience, claims management approach, and how you handle complex stakeholder situations. It's also a great opportunity to learn more about the team culture and ways of working.

3

Technical Interview — Claims Deep Dive

~60–90 minutes · In-person (London)

An in-depth technical discussion with senior members of the claims and underwriting leadership teams. You'll be assessed on your casualty claims knowledge, coverage analysis ability, reserving philosophy, and strategic thinking. Expect scenario-based questions around complex claims you've managed and your approach to managing a team through technical challenges.

4

Final Stage — Executive Panel

~45–60 minutes · In-person (London)

A final conversation with the Chief Claims Officer and one or two members of the executive team. Less formal in nature — this is an opportunity to explore mutual fit, discuss your longer-term ambitions, and ask any final questions. An offer will typically follow within a few working days of this stage.

✓

Offer & Onboarding

Offer within 48–72 hours of final stage

CRS will manage the offer process end-to-end, ensuring a smooth transition. We'll support you through resignation, notice periods, and onboarding to ensure the experience is as seamless as possible.
